About FT Flyer

This was developed by Drones Team led by Drones Head of VRMC 2017, Teo Hong Ray. This is a FT Flyer designed by Flite Test, a simple RC plane which we built from scratch out of expanded polypropylene (EPP) foam.

More about FT Flyer


The large, dihedral wings give it plenty of lift and stability, while it can pitch and yaw thanks to servos controlling the elevator and rudder respectively. Under the hood, the plane is powered by a EMAX BL2205 outrunner brushless motor hooked up to a 3 cell Lithium Polymer (LiPo) battery. The pilot is able to communicate with the plane through a FlySky FS-T6 Hobby Grade Transmitter which talks with a FS-R6B receiver attached onto the plane.

                                         

Our maiden flight was a total disaster, with the plane just crashing nose down the moment we let go of it. After some video analysis, we realised that this was due to the motor providing insufficient thrust. We replaced our battery with one of a higher voltage to supply more power and voila, the plane was in the air!

However, our troubles didn't end there. Over a few more test runs, we constantly refined our elevator and rudder to balance our controls, especially when the plane was very prone to stalling. All these tests, which often ended with with the plane crashing nose down, took a toll on the foam that started to tear and crack. Many repairs had to be made, even to the point of remaking our fuselage! Eventually, our plane was ready and we have what you see here in the video.
