About East Zone Robotics Challenge 2019

The East Zone Science and Technology Centre at VJC organized the 2019 East Zone Robotics Challenge as part of the centre's effort to inspire and create greater interest in Science, Technology, Engineering and Math during the newly revised STEM Festival (formerly known as Science Carnival@VJC). This event provides a platform for both primary and secondary school students to exercise their creativity and problem-solving skills in the field of Robotics. In total, we had 2 challenges, the Autonomous Challenge and the Exploration Challnege, each consisting of a heavy and a light category. Furthermore, 3 special awards, for Best in Mechanical Construction, Most Innovative Design and Most Efficient Robot, were awarded to 1 primary and 1 secondary school each.

For our first challenge, participants had to shoot tennis balls through holes of varying heights and sizes, cut in cardbord, using the robot they designed.

A group of winners recieving their prize.



Here is a look at the set-up for our second challenge:


For our second challenge, participants had to design a bridge that could withstand the most weight without significant bending or cracking. They had to use a 3D modelling software, which added an extra challenge to some of the groups, in addition to the ideation of the bridge design. We then printed out their bridges according to their 3D model software, and put the strength of the bridges to the test.


Training Workshop for our second challenge:



We were fortunate enough to have Ms Michelle Cheah from SUTD conduct a workshop on the basics of 3D modelling, including using a slicer software, as well as basic physics of building a strong bridge structure as a prerequisite to our second challenge. The participating groups benefitted tremendously from the comprehensive and immersive workshop.
